Fonske

Location: Rector de Somerplein (Foshplein), 3000 Leuven
Material: Bronze
Creator: Jef Claerhout
Year: 1975

The Fonske is a statue in the center of the university city of Leuven, on the Rector De Somerplein. Its full name is Fons Sapientiae, Latin for Source of Wisdom. The Fonske was designed by sculptor Jef Claerhout and was donated to the city on the occasion of the 550th anniversary of the Catholic University of Leuven in 1975. The statue represents a student who, reading a book, has the wisdom running through his head in the form of water. But this statement would later have been given to the statue. According to sculptor Claerhout: "It is a sculpture of someone who studies his behavior, pouring a lager into his head." Student organizations disagreed and felt it was a caricature of the drinking student. Therefore, the statue was renamed "Source of Wisdom".
