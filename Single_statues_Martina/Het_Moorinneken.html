Het Moorinneken

Location: Grote Markt, 3000 Leuven
Material: Bronze
Creator: Égide Rombaux
Year: 1920's

On the Grote Markt there is a café called Het Moorinneken. The name of this building is not a direct reference to Belgian Congo, but it is said to have been taken from the adjacent building that was named after the Middle Dutch Arthurian novel De Moriaen. Legend has it that a clerk confused the name 'Den Moer' with 'Den Moor'. After all, the term 'Moor' was known from the late Middle Ages and was considered a container term for persons of African origin.
When the café was rebuilt after the First World War, the Schaerbeek sculptor Égide Rombaux brightened up the facade with a bust of a Moorish beauty. This sculpture also clearly shows the influence of the then colonial iconography from the fin de siècle. The image of the Moorinneken in Leuven is therefore mainly an example of a colonial frame of reference in the early twentieth century.
