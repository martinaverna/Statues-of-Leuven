Celestial Globe

Location: Arras College, 3000 Leuven
Material: Bronze
Creator: Ferdinand Verbiest (replica)
Year: 1989

The Kangxi celestial globe is an exact replica of the instrument that the Jesuit Ferdinand Verbiest designed for the imperial court in China in 1673. The original is in the Imperial Observatory in Beijing, together with other astronomical instruments that Verbiest built. The replica was produced entirely manually according to the original techniques, with the exception of the invisible parts. It is the same size as the original but it has been made slightly heavier to minimize future distortions. The celestial globe contains 1888 celestial objects in the form of larger knots and smaller points, the stars and celestial bodies known at the end of the seventeenth century.
