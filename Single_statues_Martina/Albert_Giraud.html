Albert Giraud

Location: Sint-Donatuspark, 3000 Leuven
Material: Bronze
Creator: Victor Rousseau
Year: 1935

Émile Albert Kayenbergh, dit Albert Giraud, (Leuven June 23, 1860 - Schaerbeek December 26, 1929) was a Belgian symbolist poet of French expression, playwright and journalist.
In 1920, he became one of the first members of the Royal Academy of French Language and Literature in Belgium. Arnold Schönberg set to music 21 poems from his collection "Pierrot lunaire" (1884). Blind for the last years of his life, he died in Schaerbeek where a street is dedicated to him.
