Léon Scheurs

Location: Léon Schreursvest, 3000 Leuven
Material: Bronze
Creator: Eugène Aernauts
Year: 1922

Léon Scheurs (Molenbeek-Saint-Jean, February 10, 1888 - Louvain, August 19, 1914) was a Belgian soldier during the First World War.
At the beginning of the war, he was called up and assigned to the 6th Line Regiment. When Louvain withdrew, he was separated from the rest of his regiment but chose to stay in Louvain. At the Tiensepoort in Leuven, he managed to shoot down five German soldiers before being killed. This success made him known in history as "the last defender of Leuven".
