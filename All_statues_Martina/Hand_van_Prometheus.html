Hand van Prometheus

Location: Professor van Overstraetenplein 1, 3001 Heverlee
Material: Stainless steel
Creator: Wouter Mulier and Eeklo Cretel
Year: 2008

This artwork represents the hand of Prometheus, the Greek god and symbol of knowledge, inspiration and creativity.
In the center of the palm, the whimsical opening suggests the sparkle of the fire. By abstracting hand and fire, the work transcends the anecdotal and is translated into a visually interesting and powerful form. If the design is sleek, it stands out for its asymmetry, whimsical interplay of lines and clear use of materials. In this way, the image harmonizes with its surroundings, while on the other hand it contrasts with it. The statue (6.30 x 4.60 x 1.87 m) was commissioned by IMEC and it was made of stainless steel 316L, an extra corrosion-resistant and maintenance-friendly alloy. Thin plates (2.5 - 3 mm) were glued and welded to the inner structure of stainless steel hollow sections. The surface treatment is glossy.
