Oude Kantien

Location: Kantineplein, 3001 Heverlee
Material: Bronze

Year: 1948

The Statue (which is better known by the name of the brasserie nearby) represents Jan Pieter Minckeleers and Louis Engelbert, 6th Duke of Arenberg. Minckelers was a Dutch physicist, chemist, meteorologist and pharmacist. He studied at the University of Louvain and was a teacher there for some time. It was in Leuven that he made his most important discovery, that of coal gas.
Minckelers was the first to carry out a series of spectacular experiments with balloons filled with coal gas, which was not only combustible, but also four times lighter than air. The sculpture refers to Minckelers' first experiment on November 21st, 1783; it represents Minckelers in conversation with his patron, the Duke of Arenberg. The two figures look towards the lawn of the castle of Arenberg, from where the balloon without passenger flew away on this autumn day. This discovery laid the scientific foundations for the Zeppelins' era a hundred years later.
