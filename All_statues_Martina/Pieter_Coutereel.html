Pieter Coutereel

Location: Smoldersplein, 3000 Leuven
Material: Bronze
Creator: Georges Vandevoorde
Year: 1936

The nobleman Pieter Coutereel carried out a cup in 1360 and made Leuven the first Brabant city to breakdown the political monopoly of the aristocracy.
The city ordered the realization of the statue of Coutereel as part of the 50th anniversary of the Leuven section of the Belgian Workers Party. The artist Georges Vandevoorde depicted Coutereel dressed as a 14th century bailiff (representative of a sovereign or a lord). The statue stands outside the law courts, as a symbol of justice and equality, concepts that Coutereel espoused keenly during his life. The statue was unveiled in 1936.
