Oorlogsmonument Leuven

Location: Martelarenplein, 3000 Leuven
Material: Stone and Bronze
Creator: Jan-Albert de Bondt and Marcel Wolfers
Year: 1923-1925

With the creation of the "martyrs' monument" in 1923-1925, the city council wanted to pay dignified and ostentatious tribute to the numerous civilians who, according to the Belgian version, were innocently executed by the Germans during the August days of 1914.
Architect A. de Bondt, whose design was awarded in the public competition of 1921, conceived the monument as a representative and impressive memorial with contemporary design, characterized by a sleek geometric lines and stylized ornamentation in line with the art deco. Concrete and white natural stone were used as construction material. Marcel Wolfers, known sculptor and skilled in the applied arts (including lacquer worker and medalist), was responsible for the monumental sculptural elaboration.
