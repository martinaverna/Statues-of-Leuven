Koning Albert I


Location: Dirk Boutslaan, 3000 Leuven
Material: Bronze
Creator: Georges Jotthier
Year: 1952

Albert I was born in 1875 and was killed in an accident in Marche-les-Dames in 1934. He was crowned king in 1909, so he had to show his strength when World War I broke out. Which he also did. He is still known for his steadfast attitude as commander in chief of the Belgian troops. A quality that earned him the name 'King Soldier'. In Brussels and Bruges he is also depicted with a war helmet and a horse. The statue in Leuven was donated to the city after the war by the Association of Veterans of Leuven. It was created by the architect Georges Jotthier, who worked at the Leuven town hall. In the 1980s, the statue was thrown into the Dyle by vandals, but fortunately it could be recovered. A bust of Albert I can also be found in the facade of the university library.
