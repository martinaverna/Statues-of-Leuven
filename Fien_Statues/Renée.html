Renée

Location: Naamsestraat, 3000 Leuven
Material: Bronze
Creator: Armand Loveniers
Year: 1997

The statue is also sometimes called Fronske because she is the female counterpart of Fonske (on Fochplein) as a diligent student. Fonske is a pleasant student, Reneeke a diligent girl who goes to class or comes from the library, always with the books pressed to her heart. The statue of Renee was donated to the city of Leuven in 1997 by the Merchants' Association for his fiftieth birthday. The actual initiator was René Depret, honorary citizen of the city of Leuven, who took his name from the statue. A funny detail is that at the time only two female students at the KUL bore the name Renée. The location of the statue was also chosen by accident. The statue was placed in Naamsestraat because it was the home of the first girls' peda and because there are many university buildings to this day. The designer of the statue is artist Armand Loveniers, who specializes in life-size bronze statues.
