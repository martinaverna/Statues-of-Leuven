Mercator

Location: Mercatorpad, 3000 Leuven
Material: Bronze
Creator: Raoul Biront
Year: 2001

Mercator, best known for its world and celestial atlas, lived in the 16th century and spent many years in Leuven. Initially to study mathematics, later to work as a surveyor. At the age of 24 he married Barbara Schellekens, probably a lady from Leuven, with whom he had six children, all of whom were baptized in St. Peter's Church. Mercator lived with his family on Schrijnmakersstraat, near Mechelsestraat. The Trials College (the current Wentelsteen on the Vismarkt) was also located in that area. Mercator left Leuven at the age of 40 to settle in his native Netherlands. It was a gift from the Merchants' Association in honor of its 55th anniversary. It concerns the most authentic effigy of Mercator: with a globe and a compass . You will find another image of Mercator in the sculpture gallery of the facade of the town hall, on the side of the police station.
